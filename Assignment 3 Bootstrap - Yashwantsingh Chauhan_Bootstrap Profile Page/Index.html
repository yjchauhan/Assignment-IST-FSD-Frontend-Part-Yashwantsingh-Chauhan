Akshay Kumar

Rajiv Hari Om Bhatia, known professionally as Akshay Kumar is a Hindi-language film actor and producer who was born in India but became a naturalised Canadian citizen. Over the course of his acting career, which spans more than 30 years, he has acted in more than 100 films and has garnered numerous honours, including two National Film Awards and two Filmfare Awards. In 2009, the Indian government presented him with the Padma Shri.

M S Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni is a cricketer from India. From 2007 to 2017, he led the Indian national team in limited-overs cricket, and from 2008 to 2014, he oversaw the group in Test cricket. He is widely considered as one of cricket's greatest captains, wicket-keeper-batsman, and finishers. He is a right-handed wicket-keeper-batsman known for his calm captaincy and ability to win games in tight situations. He is also the current captain of the Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League.

Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata is a former chairman of Tata Sons and an Indian industrialist. He served as the Tata Group's chairman from 1990 to 2012, then as interim chairman from October 2016 to February 2017. He is still the chairman of its charity trusts. After obtaining the Padma Bhushan, India's third highest civilian medal, in 2000, he was awarded the Padma Vibhushan, the country's second highest civilian distinction, in 2008.

Steve Jobs

Steven Paul Jobs was an American business tycoon, inventor, and investor. He served as the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple as well as Pixar's majority shareholder, the founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT, and the chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar after that company was acquired by The Walt Disney Company. Along with his early business partner and fellow Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak, he was a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s.
